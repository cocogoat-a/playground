# Upwork Pre-Call Follow-Up System

This document turns the pre-call follow-up plan into a repeatable operating system for Etavrian's early-stage Upwork leads.

## Core Rule

Do not send a pure ping unless it is the final soft-close message.

Every follow-up should follow this shape:

`context -> micro-value -> low-friction CTA`

Examples of acceptable micro-value:

- one sharp observation from the site, ad account description, or job post
- one relevant case study proof point
- one screenshot or short Loom with a single insight
- one scoping question that helps estimate work
- one short "here is what I would check first" mini-plan

## Non-Negotiables

- Reply to new client messages within 2 hours when possible and never later than 24 hours.
- Keep communication on Upwork before contract.
- Use Upwork Messages, Upwork video, attachments, screenshots, and Loom links shared inside Upwork only.
- Do not share email, Telegram, phone, Calendly, or LinkedIn handles before contract.
- Do not promise results before reviewing the site or account.
- Do not send "just checking in", "bumping this up", or repeated copy with no new value.

## Lead Tiering

Use these four signals:

- niche fit
- budget or business maturity signal
- pain clarity
- buying intent

### Tier A

Use Tier A when at least 3 of these are true:

- strong fit for SEO, PPC, local SEO, e-commerce, SaaS, or B2B growth
- clear problem or target
- clear urgency or active conversation
- real company or real budget signal

### Tier B

Use Tier B when there is a decent fit but some uncertainty around scope, budget, or urgency.

### Tier C

Use Tier C when the fit is weak, the scope is vague, or the upside does not justify custom work before a call.

## Lead States

- `Client replied`
- `Needs answer + call CTA`
- `Waiting after value touch 1`
- `Waiting after value touch 2`
- `Soft-close / park`
- `Call booked`

## Message Cadence

| Timing | Goal | Tier A | Tier B | Tier C |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Same day as client reply | Answer fast and move toward call | Direct answer + 1 useful observation + specific-time CTA if warm | Direct answer + 1 useful observation + optional CTA | Direct answer + 1 useful observation or question |
| 24-48 hours later | Value touch 1 | 3-bullet mini-audit or short Loom/screenshot | 1 observation + 1 relevant case | 1 short observation only |
| Day 4-5 | Value touch 2 | New angle, likely blocker, or business trigger + specific-time CTA | New angle or question + next-step CTA | One final useful question or proof point if still worth it |
| Day 7-8 | Soft close | Short respectful park message | Short respectful park message | Short respectful park message |

Do not send more than three pre-call follow-ups across roughly 7-8 days unless the client re-engages.

## CTA Rules

### Use an interest CTA when the lead is exploratory

Examples:

- "Would it be useful if I send 3 quick findings first?"
- "If you want, I can take a quick look and tell you what I would check first."

### Use a specific-time CTA when the lead is warm

Examples:

- "I can do Tuesday 11:00 or Wednesday 15:00 Kyiv time in Upwork video. Would either work?"
- "If a quick call helps, I am free tomorrow at 12:00 or 16:00 Kyiv time."

## Value Asset Menu

### Tier A

- 3-bullet mini-audit
- screenshot with one annotated issue
- 60-90 second Loom
- short "month 1 focus" outline
- niche-matched case proof

### Tier B

- 1 specific observation
- 1 case snippet
- 1 scoping question
- short list of what would be checked first

### Tier C

- 1 observation
- 1 short question
- 1 proof snippet only if it directly matches the job

## LinkedIn-Informed Research

Use LinkedIn as a research input, not as a communication channel before contract.

Look for:

- recent hiring
- role changes
- new product or service launch
- expansion into new markets
- growth, lead generation, PPC, SEO, or sales posts

Turn those signals into relevant Upwork context:

- "I noticed you are hiring sales reps, so lead quality and tracking may be a bigger priority right now."
- "I saw the recent expansion into [market], which often changes landing-page and conversion-tracking priorities."

## Operating Workflow

1. Read the last client message and answer every direct question first.
2. Review the job post, site, and any files or links already shared.
3. Assign a tier: A, B, or C.
4. Choose the lightest value asset that still moves the conversation forward.
5. End with either an interest CTA or a specific-time CTA.
6. Log the touch, next due date, and value type in the tracker.
7. If there is no reply by day 7-8, send the soft-close note and park the lead.

## Quality Check Before Sending

- Is the message answering the client before selling?
- Does this follow-up add something new?
- Is the value proportional to lead quality?
- Is the CTA low-friction?
- Is everything kept on Upwork?
- Is the tone short, practical, and founder-led?

## Metrics To Track

- median first-response time
- reply rate after follow-up #1
- call-book rate by tier
- percent of leads with a value asset before the call
- percent of leads parked after day 8 with no response
